Off-Campus Accommodation in US for International Students

University Living • Last updated: January 29, 2025

The United States of America, often known as a “melting pot” of different cultures, is the third-largest country in terms of land area and is on track to becoming the third-largest in terms of population as well. Even though all 50 states in the country have the same language and government, the people living here represent diverse cultures from almost every part of the world. With its countless opportunities, the US remains a highly desired destination for international students looking to study abroad in the US.

If you’re considering studying abroad in the USA, it’s essential to take accommodation into account. The cost of accommodation varies across cities in the USA, and it’s worth noting that this country is known for being one of the pricier options for studying abroad. This is particularly important for international students who have their sights set on studying in the USA. In this article, we’ll shed light on the various off-campus student accommodation options in the US specifically for international students.

What are Off-Campus Student Accommodations?

Off-campus student accommodations refer to housing options that are not offered by the universities and are situated outside the campus area. This can include private rental apartments, homestays, shared rooms, or other rental properties where students can live while attending classes. These housing facilities provide greater flexibility and privacy compared to on-campus choices, making them an excellent option for students who prefer an independent lifestyle.

Different Types of Off-Campus Student Accommodations in US

When it comes to off-campus student accommodation in the USA, there are plenty of choices to suit every preference. From cozy private rooms and fun shared flats to modern studios and trendy apartments, you’ll find a perfect home away from home. The average rental prices for international students in the USA range from $620 to $2,000 per month. Here are some top-notch off-campus housing options you shouldn’t miss. 

PBSAs

PBSA, short for purpose-built student accommodation, is a great housing option for university students situated off-campus. These student residences are tailor-made by private developers with the specific needs of students in mind. Typically, you’ll find fully equipped studios or groups of apartments with individual kitchens, while enjoying a communal living space. Additionally, PBSAs offer a variety of housing options where you can compare and choose ensuite bedrooms, studios, and other shared spaces. So, you have plenty of options to find comfortable and convenient student accommodation.

Private Rental Apartments

There are accommodation providers that offer fully furnished private apartments in the city. These apartments come with multiple rooms, including a cozy living area, a well-equipped kitchen, and a comfortable bedroom. In some cases, you might even find apartments with two or three bedrooms, perfect for sharing with a fellow student or any other person to divide the expenses.

Shared Rooms

In shared rooms, which are commonly known as twin rooms, you’ll be living with another student in the same space. This typically includes two beds, shared storage space, and a common study area. Furthermore, you and your housemate will have the opportunity to share spaces such as the bathroom, living area, and kitchen together.

Ensuite Rooms

The ensuite rooms offer the luxury of a personal sleeping area combined with a private bathroom. With an ensuite room, you enjoy the comfort of having your own bedroom and bathroom, ensuring a peaceful and secluded experience without sharing them with others. It’s important to note that while you do have access to a common kitchen, it is shared among fellow residents. 

Studio Rooms

A studio is a cozy and convenient apartment option designed for one person. It offers a private living space with cooking facilities, storage, and a bathroom. It’s perfect for those who cherish their privacy and independence. In fact, a studio combines multiple rooms like the living room, bedroom, and kitchen into one compact unit. This means you’ll have your own sleeping area, kitchen, bathroom, and even a separate living space. It’s a perfect option for people seeking their very own cozy retreat.

Homestays

A homestay is a fantastic option for students looking for a more authentic experience. It offers you the chance to live with a local family and experience their culture firsthand. It’s perfect for independent students living abroad, or anyone who wants a genuine travel experience. Plus, it’s a cost-effective alternative that allows you to immerse yourself in the local community. 

Choosing between different options for off-campus student housing in US ultimately comes down to personal preferences and individual circumstances. Off-campus options can be a more budget-friendly choice, providing greater privacy, independence, and flexibility in terms of living arrangements. It’s important for students to evaluate their own needs, preferences, budgets, and the availability of housing options when deciding on the perfect off-campus housing for themselves.
